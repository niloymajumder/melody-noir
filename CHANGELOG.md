# Changelog

All notable changes to Melody Noir theme will be documented in this file.

## [v2.1.0] — Four More Themes Drops from Gorillaz Editions & Quality Improvements

Four additional Gorillaz album *The Mountain* (2026) interpretations, expanding the visual diversity and improved UI hierarchy:

- **Delirium** — Psychedelic chaos with hot pink strings, electric cyan constants, neon gold operators, and radiation green keywords.
- **The God of Lying** — False divinity with gold authority, jade truths, and skeptical purples exploring satire and subversion.
- **Casablanca** — Noir-dystopia with dame gold, sky blue, and sienna romance evoking mystery and cinematic tension.
- **The Manifesto** — Revolution palette with blood red strings, revolution orange constants, and militant blacks for urgency and defiance.

### Changes

- Added four new Gorillaz Edition themes
- Extended palette diversity: psychedelic, satirical, noir, and revolutionary aesthetics
- Same high-fidelity color structure (267 UI keys + 73+ scopes) as v2.0.0-v2.0.1
- Updated package.json with new theme registrations

## [v2.0.0] — Gorillaz x Melody Noir (Initial Launch)

First three themes from the Gorillaz album *The Mountain* (2026) — more songs coming in next updates:

- **Orange County** — Warm sunset California melancholy. Burnt orange, golden amber, and dusty rose over a deep warm background.
- **The Mountain** — Epic mountain ascent at misty dawn. Mountain sage, rocky amber, and sky slate blue over cool dark stone.
- **The Sad God** — Sacred cave bioluminescence. Moonlit blues, ritual fire gold, and mystic lavender over a deep blue-black void.

### Release Features

- Added three Gorillaz Edition themes under `Melody Noir: Gorillaz Editions`
- Completely redesigned The Sad God palette for deeper cave atmosphere
- Full 267-key UI color coverage + 73+ tokenColor scopes per theme
- Terminal and Git integration for all three themes
